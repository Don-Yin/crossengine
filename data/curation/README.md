# data curation

## overview

this directory contains all scripts and documentation for the asset universe used in the backtesting engine comparison study. the goal is a defensible, systematic selection that supports 30 independent, compositionally independent replications per experimental condition across diverse market sectors.

## universe selection

### sampling frame

S&P 500 constituents -- a third-party, well-understood index that provides a built-in filter for market capitalization and liquidity, removing researcher degrees of freedom from the selection process.

### selection rule

for each of the 11 GICS (Global Industry Classification Standard) sectors, we select the top 16-17 largest companies by market capitalization that satisfy:

1. continuous trading history from 2020-01-01 to 2025-01-01
2. no ticker changes or delistings during the sample period
3. listed on NYSE or NASDAQ
4. at least 1250 trading days with no NaN gaps longer than 5 days

this produces 180 stocks (4 sectors x 17 + 7 sectors x 16). the selection is mechanical and reproducible -- no discretionary stock-picking is involved. all candidate tickers were verified with yfinance before inclusion.

### why 180 stocks with non-overlapping buckets

the paper studies implementation risk (divergence between backtesting engines given identical inputs). with 180 stocks partitioned into 30 non-overlapping 6-stock buckets:

- each stock appears in exactly 1 bucket, eliminating shared-stock confounds between conditions
- 30 replications per condition, comfortably exceeding CLT-based inference thresholds
- compositional independence: no two buckets share any stock, so divergence measurements across buckets are statistically independent
- rerandomization (Morgan & Rubin, 2012) ensures balance in volatility and correlation characteristics across buckets

contrast with the previous 33-stock / 30-bucket overlapping design where stocks appeared in 3-5 buckets each, creating dependence between replications.

### sector coverage

11 GICS sectors, 16-17 stocks per sector (4 sectors at 17, 7 sectors at 16), selected as the top by market capitalization within each sector. all 180 tickers are listed in `data/universe.json`.

### acknowledged limitations

- survivorship bias: only current S&P 500 members are included. stocks delisted during 2020-2025 are excluded. this is standard in applied studies (Ledoit and Wolf, 2003; DeMiguel et al., 2009) but should be noted as a limitation.
- large-cap only: S&P 500 membership implies large capitalisation. engine divergence patterns on small-cap or micro-cap stocks (where data quality issues are more common) may differ.
- 5-year window: shorter than the 30-60 year spans in factor studies, though sufficient for engine-comparison purposes where the question is about implementation mechanics, not long-run return distributions.

## stratification (bucket design)

from the 180-stock universe, we generate 30 non-overlapping buckets of 6 stocks each via rerandomization (Morgan & Rubin, 2012). each bucket satisfies:

- exactly 6 stocks from 6 different GICS sectors (maximum sector diversity per bucket)
- each stock appears in exactly 1 bucket (non-overlapping / compositionally independent)
- zero pairwise overlap between any two buckets
- sector balance: each sector appears in 16-17 of the 30 buckets

the generation algorithm:

1. generate 20,000 candidate random partitions
2. for each candidate: randomly assign 180 stocks to 30 buckets of 6, constrained so each bucket draws from 6 distinct sectors
3. score each partition by balance criterion: Mahalanobis distance on 3 covariates (mean_ann_vol, mean_pairwise_corr, log_total_return)
4. keep the partition with the lowest balance score

the selected partition and its seed are deterministic given the candidate count and base seed.

for benchmarks requiring fewer than 6 assets (e.g. binary switch needs 2, cash-starved needs 3), the first N tickers from each bucket are used. this ensures that the same assets participate across all conditions within a given bucket, controlling for asset-selection effects.

after generation, statistical validation confirms that the 30 buckets are balanced in volatility, correlation, and sector mix. see the QC outputs for bucket diversity plots.

buckets are stored in `data/buckets.json` and generated by `data/curation/stratification.py`.

## quality control

### universe-level (`qc.py`)

`data/curation/qc.py` validates the downloaded data and generates:

- `data/qc/stats.json` -- per-ticker statistics (coverage, missing days, returns, volatility)
- `data/qc/prices.png` -- normalized price chart (log scale) for all tickers
- `data/qc/correlation-heatmap.png` -- pairwise return correlation with sector boundary lines
- `data/qc/volatility.png` -- lollipop chart of annualized volatility sorted globally
- `data/qc/missing-data.png` -- continuous coverage heatmap by month

### bucket-level (`bucket_qc.py`)

`data/curation/bucket_qc.py` validates the non-overlapping stratification design and generates:

- `data/qc/bucket-stats.json` -- non-overlap verification, chi-square, entropy, per-bucket characteristics
- `data/qc/bucket-diversity.png` -- scatter of per-bucket volatility vs correlation
- `data/qc/bucket-sector-balance.png` -- sector slot-count bar chart with chi-square test
- `data/qc/bucket-sector-cooccurrence.png` -- sector co-occurrence within buckets

all plots follow Nature formatting guidelines: 300 DPI, sans-serif fonts (7 pt body, 8 pt bold titles), top/right spines removed, colorblind-safe Wong/Tol palette.
